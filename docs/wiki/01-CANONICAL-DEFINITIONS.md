# Canonical Definitions

## 1. Purpose
Define the canonical terminology for the OVERSCITE™ ecosystem. These terms are non-negotiable and strictly enforced across all documentation and implementation surfaces.

## 2. Terminology
- **ARC (Authority identity Register & Credentialing)**: The system of record for physical human identity and accountability.
- **BANE (Boundary & Audit Network Enforcement)**: The zero-trust constraint and validation architecture.
- **BFI (Bona Fide Intelligence)**: Augmented intelligence that prioritizes truth, provenance, and human control.
- **LARI (Logical Analytical Reasoning Instrument)**: AI-driven synthesis and reasoning engine that decomposes human intent into execution plans.
- **OVERSCITE™**: The operational workspace, dashboard, and execution environment.
- **SCINGULAR™**: The orchestration architecture that integrates all cognitive and operational services.
- **Scing**: The conversational interface layer and voice-first gateway.
- **SRT (Sovereign Reality Tracking)**: The high-integrity sensor and data capture protocol.
- **TEON (Tactical Execution & Operational Network)**: The kinetic constraint arbitration layer for remote or physical device operations.
- **WIRM™ (Witnessed Interaction & Record Monitor)**: Downstream governed actuation and evidence tracking.

## 3. Principle
Absolute alignment of terminology is mandatory. If a term's meaning is diluted, BANE enforcement will identify the drift as architectural decay.
