# Chapter 19 — Reference Glossary

*Volume 10: Quick Start & Reference | OVERSCITE Global User Manual*

---

## How to Use This Glossary

This glossary defines every specialized term used in OVERSCITE and the SCINGULAR ecosystem. Terms are organized alphabetically. Canonical names are preserved in their official styling (capitalization, trademark marks, and spacing).

---

## A

**AIP (Augmented Intelligence Protocol)**  
The proprietary communication protocol that connects OVERSCITE clients to the SCINGULAR AI backend. AIP encodes roles, capabilities, and policies, enabling structured communication between the operator, Scing, LARI engines, and BANE enforcement. See Chapter 3.

**App Shell**  
The persistent layout wrapper that contains the Sidebar, Top Command Bar, Main Content Area, and OverHUD. Every authenticated page renders inside the App Shell. See Chapter 3.

**ARC (Accountable Responsible Contact)**  
The identity authority for human users in the SCINGULAR ecosystem. ARC represents the individual accountable human — distinct from OVERSCITE organizational workspace identity. ARC-prefixed identities (`arc-*`) are subject to ARC-specific BANE policy profiles.

**ArcHive™**  
The governance-anchored, witness-oriented archival and documentation continuity system within the SCINGULAR ecosystem.

**Authority Gate**  
A governance checkpoint where human approval is required before a consequential action can execute. The Inspector Authority Gate is the most prominent example. See Chapter 11.

---

## B

**BANE (Boundary, Audit, Notification, Enforcement)**  
The integrity gatekeeper of OVERSCITE. BANE validates actions at governance gates, creates immutable audit records, monitors behavioral patterns, and enforces policy profiles. See Chapters 4, 14, and 15.

**BANE-Watcher**  
The security observation protocol that monitors for anomalous behavioral patterns, policy violations, and threat indicators. Operates in isolated data collections separate from operational data. See Chapter 15.

**BFI (Bona Fide Intelligence)**  
The philosophical framework governing all SCINGULAR systems. BFI requires that intelligence be transparent, trustworthy, human-serving, and honest about limitations. "Bona fide" means "in good faith." See Chapter 2.

**Booking**  
A scheduled time block in the Calendar (`CalendarBooking`). Can be linked to an inspection, meeting, or operational block. See Chapter 13.

**Bounded Shell**  
A truth-state classification for routes with complete interface logic but backend wiring restricted to safe, predictable states. See Chapter 10.

---

## C

**Capability Token**  
A specific permission granted to an operator that authorizes a particular action. OVERSCITE uses capability-based authorization rather than role-based access control. See Chapter 14.

**[CANDIDATE]**  
Truth-state for data or outputs generated by the system but not yet reviewed and approved by a human operator. Reports, findings, and proposals start as [CANDIDATE]. See Chapter 10.

**Classification**  
The action severity level assigned by BANE: `read`, `write`, `sensitive`, or `irreversible`. Classification determines governance requirements. See Chapter 14.

**Cloud Functions**  
Server-side logic hosted on Firebase. All governed intelligence operations run as Cloud Functions, subject to BANE enforcement. See Chapter 3.

---

## D

**Deferred Shell**  
A truth-state classification for routes with visual existence only. Interactions are truthfully disabled. See Chapter 10.

**Demon Mode**  
BANE's critical threat response mechanism. Activates when threat scores exceed 90, honeytokens are accessed, or repeated violations occur. Isolates the affected session, revokes tokens, and creates incident SDRs. See Chapter 14.

---

## E

**Execution Gate**  
A conceptual surface where human approval is required before LARI-generated proposals become consequential actions. Synonymous with Authority Gate.

---

## F

**Fail-Closed**  
BANE's default behavior: when an action cannot be classified or a policy cannot be resolved, the system denies the request rather than permitting it. See Chapter 14.

**Firestore**  
The document database (Firebase Cloud Firestore) that stores all OVERSCITE operational data. Collections follow governed schemas with security rules enforcing access boundaries. See Chapter 3.

**Functional MVP**  
A truth-state classification for routes with real data-driven logic and consequential actions that are either fully functional or truthfully simulated with audit logging. See Chapter 10.

---

## G

**Governance Receipt**  
An audit record documenting a gate traversal event. Pre-gate receipts record the request arriving at the gate; post-gate receipts record the outcome. See Chapter 15.

**Guangel Safety Strip**  
A binary go/no-go safety classification displayed in the Weather Command Center. Green (CLEAR) indicates conditions within acceptable ranges; Red (DANGER) indicates one or more hazard thresholds exceeded. Named from "guardian angel." See Chapter 7.

---

## H

**Hardening Map**  
A document tracking the depth of BANE governance integration across all action paths in the system. Classifies paths as fully wrapped, endpoint-protected, or not yet implemented. See Chapter 14.

**Human Authority Doctrine**  
The foundational architectural constraint: no component may self-authorize a meaningful action; all consequential execution requires explicit human approval. See Chapter 2.

---

## I

**IRI (Inspection Risk Index)**  
A composite safety metric that weights weather variables (wind, precipitation, temperature, humidity, UV) according to their impact on field work safety and measurement accuracy. Unique to OVERSCITE. See Chapter 7.

**Inspector Authority Gate**  
The interface component where inspectors review AI-generated advisory blocks, add professional notes, confirm attestation checkboxes, and digitally sign inspection reports. See Chapter 11.

---

## L

**LARI (Logistics, Analytics, Routing, Ingestion)**  
The bounded reasoning fabric of OVERSCITE. A collection of specialized engines, each following the `LariEngineContract` interface. LARI computes, analyzes, and generates — but never decides. See Chapter 4.

**[LIVE]**  
Truth-state for fully operational, real-data-connected features. See Chapter 10.

---

## M

**M-UCB (Monitoring Universal Control Block)**  
The protocol family governing BANE-Watcher security event contracts. Defines structured event formats with mandatory trust and truth-state metadata.

**[MOCK]**  
Truth-state for synthetic data provided for structural simulation and UI validation. Not suitable for professional decisions. See Chapter 10.

---

## N

**Non-Autonomous Design**  
The architectural principle that OVERSCITE is built to be maximally helpful without being independently consequential. The system proposes; the human approves. See Chapter 2.

---

## O

**OverHUD (Operational Verification Heads-Up Display)**  
The full-height right-side panel that displays system status, security alerts, and operational intelligence. Passive observation surface — does not accept user input. See Chapter 5.

**OVERSCITE**  
The governed operational environment designed to assist human decision-making in inspection, compliance, and field operations. The encompassing platform that integrates Scing, LARI, BANE, SRT, Weather, Locations, and all operational workflows.

**Oversight Chain**  
The visual representation of hierarchical relationships between project parties (prime contractors, subcontractors). Each node is an independent verification locus — compliance is not inherited. See Chapter 12.

---

## P

**[PARTIAL]**  
Truth-state for features with partially connected interfaces and data sources. See Chapter 10.

**Page Identity Band**  
The horizontal strip below the Top Command Bar displaying page title, mission context, and truth-state badge. See Chapter 6.

**Pipeline Context**  
The execution metadata attached to an SRT pipeline run, including inspection ID, inspector ID, and node metadata. See Chapter 9.

**Policy Profile**  
A named BANE configuration that determines enforcement behavior (strict mode, default verdict, escalation, quarantine, lockout, incident creation). See Chapter 14.

---

## R

**Roof Surface Temperature Model**  
An estimation model in the Weather Command Center that predicts surface temperatures for common roofing materials based on ambient conditions, solar radiation, and material properties. See Chapter 7.

---

## S

**Scing™**  
The conversational interface presence and orchestration coordinator of the SCINGULAR ecosystem. Scing interprets operator intent, coordinates LARI engines, and delivers responses. Scing does not reason, enforce, or decide. Pronounced "sing." See Chapter 4.

**Scing Panel**  
The drop-down conversational surface embedded in the Top Command Bar. Interactive surface for operator–system dialogue. See Chapter 5.

**SCINGULAR™**  
The broader sovereign system and architecture. The ecosystem within which OVERSCITE, Scing, LARI, BANE, and SRT operate. Built on the philosophy of Bona Fide Intelligence.

**SDR (Security Decision Record)**  
The atomic unit of the BANE audit trail. Created for every governance gate evaluation. Contains timestamp, user identity, action, decision, and provenance metadata. Designed to be immutable. See Chapter 15.

**SmartSCHEDULER™**  
OVERSCITE's scheduling intelligence system, accessed through the Calendar route. Provides weather-aware scheduling proposals with dependency tracking. See Chapter 13.

**SRT (Secure Remote Telemetry)**  
The six-stage data ingestion pipeline: capture → normalize → tolerance → threshold → template → audit. Ensures sensor data enters OVERSCITE in a validated, standardized, and auditable form. See Chapter 9.

---

## T

**Top Command Bar**  
The fixed horizontal bar at the top of every page containing the sidebar trigger, Scing Panel, and utility actions. See Chapters 5 and 6.

**Trace ID**  
A correlation identifier linking related SDRs, governance receipts, and LARI engine invocations into a single reconstructable event sequence. See Chapter 15.

**Truth State**  
A maturity label assigned to data-bearing surfaces: [LIVE], [PARTIAL], [MOCK], [CANDIDATE], or [ACCEPTED]. Communicates the reliability and provenance of displayed information. See Chapter 10.

---

## U

**UTCB (Universal Telemetry Control Block)**  
The declarative configuration that defines SRT pipeline parameters including tolerance schema, precision standards, and propagation rules. See Chapter 9.

---

## V

**Verification State**  
A contractor party's compliance position: `verified_active`, `verified_registered_only`, `unverified`, `expired`, or `suspended`. See Chapter 12.

---

## W

**Weather Command Center**  
The environmental intelligence system located at `/weather`. Provides current conditions, IRI risk scoring, roof surface temperature modeling, Guangel safety classification, and trend analysis. See Chapter 7.

**WIRM™ (Workflow Integrated Response Module)**  
Downstream governed actuation within the SCINGULAR ecosystem. Not free agency — operates under BANE governance constraints.

---

## 5-Zone Model

The structural template that governs every OVERSCITE route's layout: Zone 1 (Top Command Bar), Zone 2 (Page Identity Band), Zone 3 (Primary Content Region), Zone 4 (Contextual Intelligence Panel), Zone 5 (Action Surface). See Chapter 6.

---

*This glossary covers all canonical terms used in the OVERSCITE Global User Manual. For terms specific to the broader SCINGULAR ecosystem that are not directly used in OVERSCITE operations, consult the SCINGULAR Ecosystem Reference documentation.*
