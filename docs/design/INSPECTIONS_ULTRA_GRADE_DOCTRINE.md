## Preamble

This document records the operational and intelligence doctrines governing the
OVERSCITE Inspections division as the Ultra-Grade implementation standard.

These doctrines are not aspirational. They are implementation constraints that
every new feature, engine, UI surface, and governance structure must satisfy
before being considered production-ready for the Inspections division.

---

## Doctrine 1 — Inspections Is The Unified Field Intelligence Division

**Statement:**
Inspections is the single sovereign OVERSCITE division for field inspection, evidence review,
site-state observation, incident and hazard documentation, and governed recognition-driven
analysis across residential, commercial, industrial, site, insurance, safety, and
field-operator workflows.

**Hard Rules:**
- Do not fragment these workflows into disconnected weak pages
- Do not demote Inspections to report-list CRUD behavior
- Inspections must act as a command environment, not a commodity form page

---

## Doctrine 2 — Recognition Is Baseline, Not Premium Existence

**Statement:**
Baseline analysis must perform exhaustive whole-scene interrogation and best-effort
recognition across all visible scene elements. Subscription tiers may scale depth,
specialization, enrichment, correlation, and automation — but not the existence of
baseline recognition itself.

**Hard Rules:**
- Standard tier includes broad object and condition recognition — not a crippled toy-pass
- Premium tiers scale depth, taxonomy detail, and cross-context intelligence
- No baseline tier may be intentionally hobbled to upsell premium recognition

---

## Doctrine 3 — Fine-Tooth-Comb Inspection Analysis

**Statement:**
Whenever analysis is invoked, the recognition stack must interrogate the scene end-to-end
rather than only targeting the most obvious object or defect.

**Hard Rules:**
- Whole-scene interrogation is mandatory
- Secondary objects, obstructions, and background context may not be ignored
- Unknown elements must be surfaced, not silently skipped
- Visibility limits must be disclosed explicitly

---

## Doctrine 4 — Living Entities and Pest Evidence Are In Scope

**Statement:**
Living entities and pest-related evidence are inspection-relevant contextual classes
and must be recognized as part of standard image analysis.

**Hard Rules:**
- Humans, children, pets, service animals, wildlife, and vegetation are valid scene classes
- Pests, droppings, frass, mud tubes, webbing, nests, chew patterns, and infestation signatures
  are valid inspection classes
- Occlusion caused by living entities must trigger visibility-limited or obstruction truth states

---

## Doctrine 5 — Drafting and Design Intelligence Are First Class

**Statement:**
Drafting, drawing, diagram, plan, schematic, and design-engineering artifact recognition
are first-class governed intelligence classes inside the broader recognition stack.
They must support standard drawing interpretation under governed knowledge constraints.

**Hard Rules:**
- Architectural, structural, MEP, site, schematic, and detail drawings are supported reference classes
- Title blocks, symbols, revision markers, dimensions, notes, callouts, and sheet structure must be recognized
- Drafting intelligence must bind to standards and precision domain stacks rather than freeform guessing
- GD&T must be modeled as structured artifacts, not prose

---

## Doctrine 6 — Observation, Identification, Authority Separation

**Statement:**
Recognition output must preserve a three-layer truth structure:
deterministic observation, probabilistic identification, and human final authority.

**Hard Rules:**
- Observed layer uses direct language — "Staining visible on ceiling surface"
- Identified-as layer uses qualified language — "Consistent with possible water intrusion"
- Human authority remains final for all consequential determinations
- No direct jump from pattern recognition to authoritative claim

---

## Doctrine 7 — Selective Analysis and Compute Discipline

**Statement:**
The system must conserve compute by analyzing only explicitly requested or authorized media,
while still making accepted but unanalyzed media usable and truthful.

**Hard Rules:**
- No silent background fan-out analysis
- Analysis request is attributable and auditable — requestedBy + requestedAt mandatory
- Accepted media remains placeable without implying analytical completion
- Verification tags require completed governed analysis path

---

## Doctrine 8 — ArcHive™ and BANE Govern All Stack Evolution

**Statement:**
Recognition-stack logic, taxonomy expansion, model routing, source integration, and deployment
are governed authoring events inside ArcHive™ and may not deploy without BANE validation
and human approval.

**Hard Rules:**
- Required lifecycle: Proposal → Review → Validate → Approve → Deploy → Monitor → Rollback
- No silent live mutation of production stack logic
- Learning-derived expansions remain proposal-bound
